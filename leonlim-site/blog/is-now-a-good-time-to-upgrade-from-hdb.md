Every week, at least three people ask me some version of the same question.

*"Leon, is now a good time to upgrade?"*

It's a fair question. And I understand why people ask it — property is the biggest financial decision most Singaporeans will ever make. You want to get the timing right.

But here's the honest truth: **timing the market is the wrong question entirely.**

## Why timing is not the point

When people ask about timing, they're usually hoping I'll say one of two things:

1. *"Yes, now is great — buy before prices go higher."*
2. *"Not yet — wait for prices to drop."*

Neither answer is useful, because neither is really about *you*.

The right question is not "is the market ready?" It's "**am I ready?**"

## What readiness actually looks like

In my decade of advising HDB homeowners, the clients who make the best property decisions share a few things in common:

- They understand their CPF balances and what's available for a private purchase
- They've stress-tested their finances at higher interest rate scenarios
- They know whether they need to sell their HDB first — and the tax implications of getting this sequence wrong
- They've thought about their exit, not just their entry

None of these things have anything to do with where the market is today.

## The cost of waiting for the "perfect" time

Here's what I've seen happen to families who wait for the perfect window:

The window never comes — or by the time they feel "certain," prices have already moved. Meanwhile, the gap between HDB values and private property values quietly widens. Year by year. Without drama. Without a headline.

This is what I call the **Silent Wealth Gap** — and it's the most expensive mistake I see HDB homeowners make.

## So what should you do?

Start with clarity on your own position. Not the market's position — yours.

- What are your CPF balances today?
- What is your current outstanding HDB loan?
- What's your household income and TDSR headroom?
- Where do you want to be in 10 years?

Once you have honest answers to these, the "is now a good time?" question answers itself.
